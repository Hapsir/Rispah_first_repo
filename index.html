The African Savannah

The African savannah is a transitional grassland ecosystem characterized by a continuous layer of tall grasses and widely spaced, scattered trees that do not form a closed canopy. It is home to a vast and diverse community of wildlife.

The Big Five

The “Big Five” animals are a group of large African mammals that were originally named by hunters because they were the most difficult and dangerous to hunt on foot.

  1. Elephant
  2. Lion
  3. Buffalo
  4. Rhino
  5. Leopard

Fun Facts

  • Elephants walk on their tip toes.
  • The lionesses are the hunters and primary caretakers of the pride's cubs.
  • Buffaloes often have birds on their backs that eat ticks and parasites.
  • Rhinoceros have poor vision but a keen sense of smell and hearing.
  • Leopards are the strongest of the big cats.

The Wildebeest Migration

This is a spectacular, annual, clockwise movement of over 1.5 million wildebeest, zebra, and gazelle across the Serengeti-Mara ecosystem.
